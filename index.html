Welcome to my personal website and project portfolio! My name is Sami El Sabri, public health analyst, epidemiologist, and data scientist. I am a Clarendon Scholar at the University of Oxford finishing an MSc in Global Health Science & Epidemiology, with a background in Public Health and Computational Cognitive Science from the University of Toronto. My work sits at the intersection of health equity, climate change, and migration. I specialize in turning complex, messy data into equitable, evidence-based decisions through R, Python, and GIS. Having lived and worked across three continents and traveled to 60 countries, I bring a global, multilingual perspective to local challenges. Here you find is a curated collection of my endeavours across quantitative applications, interdisciplinary collaborations, community service, and enriching travel experiences, that have helped me learn and grow as a young professional. Thanks for visiting, I hope you enjoy!
