# Renewable Energy Transition: A Decade of Progress in Toronto
**Urban Sustainability · Toronto, Canada · 2024**  
`R` · `GIS` · `Open Data Toronto`

### Analyzing Urban Sustainability & City-Owned Infrastructure

**The Project:** Utilizing the **City of Toronto Open Data Portal**, I conducted a longitudinal study (2010–2019) of renewable energy installations on city-owned facilities to evaluate Toronto's progress toward its carbon neutrality commitments[cite: 257, 266].
The report was published as a blog post on [Open Data Toronto)](https://open.toronto.ca/a-decade-of-progress-on-city-owned-buildings-and-facilities/).

**Strategic Insights:**
* **Diversification:** Analysis shows a shift from large-scale **FIT** programs to more localized **MicroFIT** systems, balancing the city's energy portfolio.
* **Equitable Expansion:** QGIS mapping reveals a deliberate strategy to expand renewable infrastructure beyond the downtown core into peripheral areas like **Scarborough and North York**[cite: 279].
* **Policy Alignment:** This research directly informs discussions regarding the city's **TransformTO Net Zero Strategy**.
